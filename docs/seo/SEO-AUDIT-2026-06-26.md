3. **🟢 The product itself converts.** 27.5% scored→paid at $6.95, 6 active
   $19.95/mo subs, 99% scoring reliability, honestly-harsh verdicts. The
   economics work *when traffic arrives* — which is exactly why #1 is the
   priority.

## 2. What's working

- **The paid wedge.** 27.5% scored→paid at $6.95 is a strong micro-conversion;
  the sealed-teaser → unlock model is doing its job.
- **AI reliability.** 99% of submitted reports score successfully.
- **Verdict credibility.** Harsh, well-spread verdicts (only 5% release-ready)
  make the score feel earned, not flattering.
- **Repeat usage.** 40 artists, 110 reports — people come back.
- **Technical SEO foundation is solid** (see §3): single www canonical, full
  sitemap, self-canonicals, rich JSON-LD (Organization / SoftwareApplication w/
  pricing / WebSite SearchAction), FAQPage + BreadcrumbList on genre pages,
  Article + FAQPage on blog, `llms.txt` present, IndexNow wired.
- **Content depth.** ~37 published URLs in the content layer (20 blog posts in
  the calendar's DONE log + alternatives + the AI-tools post), 32 genre pages,
  9 alternatives pages.

## 4. Gaps & recommendations (prioritized)

**P0 — funnel (blocks everything; not SEO but gates SEO ROI)**
- Verify logged-out `/submit-score` → `/api/score/start` still works post-auth-overhaul. The created=0 cliff is the single most important number in this audit.
- Close the IN_REVIEW→COMPLETED gap (104 stuck) so the room-of-5 actually finishes reports.

**P1 — SEO quick wins (documented; out of scope this pass per the "plan + calendar + image only" decision)**
- Add `/feedback` and `/alternatives` hub pages (index + internal-link spokes). Biggest structural SEO gap — 41 child pages with no parent.
- Add BreadcrumbList JSON-LD to blog posts.
- Add explicit AI-crawler allow rules in robots (GPTBot, PerplexityBot, ClaudeBot, Google-Extended) — protect GEO/AI-citation upside.
- Sync the IndexNow webhook to include genres + alternatives, and trigger it on publish (currently manual).

**P2 — content & authority (recurring)**
- Refilled blog queue (16 topics) now live in `seo/content-calendar.md`.
- Per-page OG images for genre/alternatives.
- Reddit/community presence (help-first) for referring domains.
- Monthly GEO/AI-citation check (already scheduled).

## 5. What to expect (trajectory)

- **Indexing/ranking:** the content layer is young (most pages < 4 weeks old).
  Expect genre + "is my song good / ready" intent pages to start surfacing in
  positions 8-20 over the next 4-8 weeks, then climb as internal linking + freshness compound. Hub pages would accelerate this.
- **Organic → revenue:** at the current 27.5% scored→paid, even modest organic
  submit volume monetizes well. The lever is **submissions**, not conversion.
  Fix the funnel cliff and organic content has somewhere to convert.
- **Honest ceiling:** without real GSC data we're flying on structure +
  inference. Wire up Search Console (see §6) to replace this with measured
  positions and find the position 8-15 "one title tweak = page-one" wins.

## 8. Real Search Console findings (last 3 months)

**Volume:** ~210 clicks · ~919 impressions · CTR ~23% (flattered by brand).
Mobile converts (55% CTR / 252 imp); desktop is the discovery pool (10% CTR /
654 imp). Strong US/UK/AU spread.

**1. 🟡 75% of clicks are branded.** "mixreflect" alone = **153 clicks @ pos
1.02**; "mix reflect" +5. Non-branded organic discovery is minimal — the brand
is doing nearly all the click-pulling. Growing non-brand clicks is the whole game.

**2. www/non-www signal split (healing, not a bug).** The index still lists both
hosts separately — homepage `mixreflect.com` (185 clicks, pos 2.03) vs
`www.mixreflect.com` (19 clicks, pos 7.6); also `/discover`, and several blog +
alternatives pages dual-listed. **Redirect verified live as 308 Permanent
apex→www**, so this is expected lag from the June-9 migration (the 3-mo window
spans the pre-fix non-www era). It will consolidate as Google recrawls; the
non-www URLs carry mostly historical clicks. Monitor; optionally nudge via a GSC
"validate fix" on duplicate-canonical.

**3. 🟢 Biggest quick win — page-1 zero-click content (CTR problem, not rank):**
- `/blog/best-music-feedback-platforms-2026`: **228 imp combined, pos ~6.5, 0
  clicks.** Rewrite title + meta description for click appeal; nudge toward pos 3.
- `/blog/how-music-producers-get-their-tracks-reviewed`: **121 imp, pos ~4, 0
  clicks.** Same play.
These already rank on page 1 and get *nothing* — pure CTR upside.

**4. Commercial-intent alternatives sit on page 2:**
- "submithub alternatives" — pos **19.7** (13 imp); `/alternatives/submithub`
  pos 7-14. "submithub alternative" pos 12.25.
- "groover alternatives" pos 13 · "groover alternative" pos 26 · "best
  alternatives to groover" pos 9.5; `/alternatives/groover` = 87 imp, pos 5.78,
  1 click. High-intent — strengthening these to page 1 brings qualified traffic.

**5. Genre pages rank well but starve for impressions.** Many `/feedback/*` sit
at pos 2-5 (ambient 2.0, alternative 2.1, soul 2.8, rock 3.0) but with single-
digit impressions — the exact long-tail "[genre] feedback" terms are thin. A
`/feedback` hub + broader genre-query targeting would feed them.

**6. Position 8-15 "one tweak = page one" targets:** music feedback ai (24),
mix feedback (25), music feedback discord (17), rate my mix (36), submithub
alternatives (19.7), groover alternative (26). Mostly need a dedicated/stronger
page or on-page tuning.

**7. Noise: "nova reyes" artist-name queries** (~28 imp, 0 clicks across
bandcamp/apple/spotify variants) — a demo/sample artist name got indexed
(likely via `/discover`). Irrelevant; confirm no indexable demo report is
leaking and consider noindex if so.

**8. Trend:** impressions climbed through June (content indexing) but clicks
*fell* and avg position drifted from ~1.3 (May, mostly brand) to ~5-12 (June, as
it ranks for harder non-brand terms). The late-May click spike (24-28/day, ~Reddit/
launch) faded. Net: indexing is working; the gap is non-branded CTR + authority.
